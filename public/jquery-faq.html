FAQ - Arches National Park

1. Can I reserve a campsite?
Yes. In fact, from March through October, individual sites at Devils Garden Campground must be reserved in advance.
2. What facilities does the campground have?
Devils Garden has 52 sites with fire rings, picnic tables, bathrooms and running water (no showers) . RV Hookups are not available.
3. Can I backpack in Arches?
Yes, although there just a few areas where backpacking is allowed.
4. What about winter camping?
During the winter (November 1 through February 28), 24 campsites are available on a first-come, first-served basis.
5. What's the maximum RV length in the campground?
Some sites will accommodate RVs up to 30 feet long.
6. When was Arches established?
On April 12, 1929, Arches became a National Monument. On November 12, 1971, Arches became a National Park.
7. How big is the park?
76,519 acres or 119 square miles.
8. How many arches are in the park?
No one knows for sure. Over 2,000 have been recorded. Some may have fallen and new arches may yet be forming.
9. What is the largest arch? What is the tallest arch?
The light opening beneath Landscape Arch is 306 feet (93.3 meters) across. Double Arch South has a vertical light opening of 144 feet (43.8 meters).
10. How many visitors come to Arches every year?
In 2010, the park received over one million visitors.
